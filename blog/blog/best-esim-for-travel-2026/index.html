1. What is an eSIM?

An eSIM (embedded SIM) is a digital SIM card built directly into your device - no physical card to swap, no plastic to lose at the airport. You download a connectivity plan online and activate it in minutes, even before you board your flight.

For travelers, this means no more hunting for a local SIM kiosk on arrival, no roaming charges from your home carrier, and the ability to keep your regular number active while using a local data plan simultaneously.

Compatible devices: iPhone XR and newer, most Android phones from 2020 onwards (Samsung Galaxy S20+, Google Pixel 3+, and many more). Check your provider's app to confirm compatibility before purchasing.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use my regular SIM and eSIM at the same time?
Yes. Most modern smartphones support dual SIM - your physical SIM and eSIM can be active simultaneously. You can keep your home number for calls and texts while using the eSIM for data. Just set your eSIM as the default for mobile data in your phone settings.
What happens when my data runs out?
Each provider handles this differently. Airalo lets you top up directly in the app. Saily requires you to purchase a new plan. Yesim offers auto-renewal options and the Pay and Fly plan so you only pay for what you use. None of them charge surprise overage fees - data simply stops when the plan is exhausted.
Does eSIM work on my phone?
eSIM is supported on iPhone XR and newer, Samsung Galaxy S20 and newer, Google Pixel 3 and newer, and most flagship Android phones from 2020 onwards. One important note: your device must be carrier-unlocked to use a third-party eSIM. Check with your carrier if unsure.
Is eSIM safe to use?
Yes, eSIM is actually more secure than a physical SIM. There is no physical card to lose, steal, or clone. Your data is stored securely in the device's chip. Saily and Yesim add additional security layers with their built-in VPN and web protection features.
Can I share my eSIM data via hotspot?
Yes - all three providers (Airalo, Saily, Yesim) support hotspot on most plans. Yesim's Pay and Fly plan may have restrictions on some networks. Always check the specific plan details before purchasing if hotspot is important to you.
