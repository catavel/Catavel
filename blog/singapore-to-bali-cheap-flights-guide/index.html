Singapore to Bali Cheap Flights Guide 2026

Everything you need to know about flying SG to Bali without overpaying.

Singapore to Bali (Denpasar, DPS) is one of the most popular short-haul routes in Southeast Asia - and one of the most price-volatile. The same return ticket can cost SGD 130 if you book right, or SGD 450 if you book wrong. This guide covers everything you need to know to consistently land on the cheaper end.

Average Flight Prices Singapore to Bali

The SIN to DPS route is served by both budget and full-service carriers, and prices vary dramatically by season, lead time, and carrier. Here is what to expect:

Best Airlines for Singapore to Bali

Scoot is the most popular choice and typically the cheapest option. It operates direct SIN-DPS flights daily, and fares start from around SGD 60-80 one way on sale. The catch: baggage, seat selection, and meals are all extras. If you pack light and book early, Scoot is hard to beat. Book directly via Scoot or compare on Trip.com for bundle options with your hotel.

AirAsia sometimes operates SIN-DPS directly, and at other times routes via Kuala Lumpur. Via KL adds 3-5 hours to your journey but can be meaningfully cheaper during peak season. If you are combining a Bali trip with a KL stopover, AirAsia via KL makes sense. Check the total journey time before committing.

Singapore Airlines flies direct and includes checked baggage, meals, and entertainment. The premium is roughly SGD 100-200 more than Scoot return, which is worth it if you value comfort, carry lots of luggage, or are traveling with children who benefit from the service. Singapore Airlines sale fares occasionally bring it close to budget carrier territory - worth monitoring.

Garuda Indonesia operates a direct SIN-DPS service. Full-service, includes baggage, occasionally surprises with competitive pricing particularly during Indonesian school holidays when Garuda runs promotions for outbound Indonesians. Worth including in your fare comparison.

Jetstar operates some SIN-DPS flights. Compare with Scoot - pricing is often similar, so go with whichever has the better schedule for your dates. Also try CheapOair for a broader fare comparison across all carriers.

Cheapest Months to Fly

February and March are consistently the cheapest months for SIN to DPS. School holidays are over, the Bali wet season is easing, and demand is at its annual low. May is another strong value month - between the Chinese New Year rush and the June school holiday surge. October and early November are similarly quiet and cheap, with excellent weather.

The most expensive periods are June to August (Singapore and Malaysian school holidays), and mid-December to early January (Christmas and New Year). If you must travel during these windows, booking 8-10 weeks in advance is essential to avoid the worst fare spikes.

One Bali-specific note: Nyepi, the Balinese Day of Silence (usually in March), results in a full 24-hour shutdown of the island including the airport. No flights land or depart on Nyepi day. Check the exact date before booking in March - some travelers intentionally plan around it to experience the unique atmosphere, but you need to be already on the island when it begins.

How to Find the Cheapest Fares

The single biggest lever is booking 6-8 weeks in advance for non-peak travel. For peak periods, extend that to 10-12 weeks. Last-minute fares on this route are almost never a bargain - Bali is too popular for unsold seats to accumulate.

Booking a Trip.com flight + hotel bundle can save an additional 10-15% versus booking flight and hotel separately on different platforms. This works best when both elements are in the same transaction - the bundle discount applies at checkout.

Tuesday and Wednesday departures are typically 10-15% cheaper than Friday or Sunday departures on the same route. If your schedule allows flexibility, this adds up significantly over a return trip.

What to Book Before You Go

Your airport transfer from Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) to your accommodation is the first thing to sort. If you land without a pre-booked transfer, you will face a fixed-rate taxi queue (overpriced) or Grab (which requires Indonesian SIM data to function). The easiest solution: book your transfer via Klook before departure. Klook private transfers from DPS airport to Seminyak or Kuta run from around SGD 10-15 and include a driver waiting with your name sign - no negotiation required.

Bali Budget Tips from Catavel

Canggu and Ubud offer dramatically better value than Seminyak or Legian for accommodation. A comfortable private villa in Canggu with a pool runs IDR 400,000-700,000 per night (SGD 30-55), while a similar property in Seminyak can cost double. The trade-off is distance from the beach and club strip - a non-issue for most travelers who prefer Bali's cultural and culinary side.

Renting a scooter costs around IDR 70,000-100,000 per day (SGD 5-8) and gives you complete flexibility to explore. If you are not comfortable on a scooter, Grab is available throughout south Bali and is significantly more honest on pricing than traditional taxis - though surge pricing during rain can make it expensive for short hops.

Eating at warungs (local Indonesian restaurants) rather than tourist-facing restaurants keeps food costs to IDR 30,000-60,000 per meal (SGD 2.50-5). Nasi campur, gado-gado, and mie goreng from a good warung beat most tourist-restaurant food on both price and taste.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest airline from Singapore to Bali?
Scoot is consistently the cheapest direct carrier on the SIN-DPS route. Sale fares start from around SGD 60-80 one way. AirAsia can occasionally undercut Scoot via Kuala Lumpur, but the longer journey time makes it less attractive unless you are combining with a KL stopover.
How far in advance should I book SG to Bali flights?
For off-peak travel: 6-8 weeks in advance hits the sweet spot between availability and price. For peak periods (June-August, December): 10-12 weeks minimum. Last-minute deals are rare on this route - Bali's popularity means seats are seldom unsold close to departure.
Do I need a visa to visit Bali from Singapore?
Singapore passport holders receive a 30-day visa-free entry to Indonesia including Bali. Malaysian and Hong Kong passport holders are also visa-free for 30 days. Philippines passport holders may need a Visa on Arrival (VOA) - check the Indonesian immigration website for current requirements before traveling.
Is it cheaper to fly to Bali on weekdays?
Yes. Tuesday and Wednesday departures are typically 10-15% cheaper than Friday or Sunday departures on the Singapore to Bali route. If you can arrange a midweek departure and return, the savings on a return ticket can be SGD 30-60 - meaningful on a budget trip.
Should I book a Bali airport transfer in advance?
Yes, strongly recommended. The fixed-rate taxi zone at Ngurah Rai airport charges premium prices (IDR 150,000-300,000 for short rides), and Grab requires an Indonesian SIM card or active data to function on arrival. Pre-booking a Klook private transfer before you leave Singapore gives you a fixed price, a named driver, and no arrival stress.
