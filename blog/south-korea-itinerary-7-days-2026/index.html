Your perfect week in South Korea, day by day.

This 7-day South Korea itinerary has been structured specifically for SEA travelers - accounting for realistic flight times, budget expectations, and the booking platforms that work best in our region.

Whether you are planning this South Korea 7 day travel plan for solo travel, a couple, or a family, this guide covers the practical details: what to see each day, how to get around, what it costs, and what to book in advance. Use it as your complete week-long Korea itinerary from start to finish.

South Korea Itinerary 7 Days - Overview

The structure of this 7-day South Korea itinerary: four days in Seoul (palaces, culture, food, day trips), two days in Busan (beaches, seafood, temples), with optional Jeju Island extension for travelers with 9-10 days total. This korea travel itinerary works equally well as a solo trip, couple's getaway, or family trip - the flexibility comes from choosing between the DMZ tour or Nami Island on Day 3.

Day 1-2, Seoul - City Highlights

Day 1 of your south korea itinerary 7 days starts at Incheon International Airport (ICN). The AREX express train runs to Seoul Station in 43 minutes (KRW 9,500). Get a T-money card at the airport - the stored-value card that works on subways, buses, and convenience stores across Korea. Load KRW 30,000-50,000 to start.

Head to Myeongdong for your first evening - Seoul's most accessible shopping and street food district. Tteokbokki, hotteok, and Korean cosmetics everywhere. The Cheonggyecheon Stream running through the city center is a pleasant 30-minute walk to decompress after the flight.

Day 2 is the historical Seoul day. Gyeongbokgung Palace opens at 9am - arrive early to catch the Changing of the Guard ceremony at 10am and 2pm. Immediately behind the palace is Bukchon Hanok Village, a hillside neighborhood of traditional Korean wooden houses. Gwangjang Market for dinner: famous for mung bean pancakes (bindaetteok) and yukhoe (Korean beef tartare). Budget KRW 15,000-25,000 for a proper market dinner.

Cap Day 2 at N Seoul Tower on Namsan Mountain. Book your cable car ticket via Klook to skip the queue and save KRW 2,000-3,000 versus counter price. Check our Klook promo codes page for additional discounts on Seoul activities.

Day 3-4, Seoul - Deeper Exploration

Day 3 of this south korea itinerary 7 days is ideal for the DMZ (Demilitarized Zone) tour - one of the most significant experiences available anywhere on a Korea trip. The DMZ can only be visited with a licensed tour operator. Book via Klook - half-day and full-day options from SGD 35-55 per person. The full-day JSA (Joint Security Area) tour, where soldiers from both sides stand face to face at the border, is worth the extra cost when available.

Alternatively, swap the DMZ for a Nami Island day trip - the scenic island made famous by the Korean drama Winter Sonata. About 1.5 hours from Seoul by train and ferry, bookable on Klook with entry bundled at a discount. This swap works particularly well if you are following a korea winter itinerary, when Nami's tree-lined paths are stunning in snow.

Day 4: Hongdae is Seoul's youth culture hub - street performers, indie music venues, and late-night food. End the evening at Han River Park: Yeouido or Ttukseom for a convenience store picnic watching the city lights. Korean BBQ dinner on Day 4 is essential. Samgyeopsal at a local restaurant in Mapo costs KRW 12,000-18,000 per portion. Budget KRW 30,000-50,000 per person for a full BBQ dinner with banchan side dishes and soju.

Day 5-6, Busan

The KTX high-speed train from Seoul to Busan covers 325km in approximately 2.5 hours. Tickets cost KRW 59,800 standard class - book via Trip.com or the KORAIL website a few days ahead. Morning departures on weekends sell out fast. Check our Trip.com Singapore promo codes before booking your tickets.

Gamcheon Culture Village is the first Busan stop - a hillside neighborhood of brightly painted houses known as the "Machu Picchu of Busan." Entry is free, with a small stamp card activity for KRW 2,000. Jagalchi Fish Market is an essential Busan experience: live seafood on the ground floor, restaurants upstairs where you can have your purchase cooked immediately. Arrive by 10am for the best selection. Budget KRW 30,000-60,000 for a seafood meal for two.

Day 6 in Busan: Haeundae Beach - Korea's most famous beach - is wide, clean, and worth a morning. Walk the Dongbaekseom coastal path adjacent to the beach for a quieter perspective. The unmissable second stop: Haedong Yonggungsa Temple, a Buddhist temple built on coastal rocks with waves crashing below. Arrive before 9am to avoid the crowds and to catch the best light for photos. There is no entry fee.

Optional Extension - Jeju Island

A Jeju extension suits travelers with 9-10 days for their South Korea travel itinerary. Fly from Busan or Seoul to Jeju International Airport (CJU) on Jeju Air or T'way - book via Trip.com. Jeju highlights: Hallasan Mountain hike, Manjanggul Cave, Seongsan Ilchulbong sunrise peak, and black pork (heukdwaeji) for dinner. Jeju requires a rental car to explore properly - arrange an International Driving Permit before leaving home.

Frequently Asked Questions

How many days do you need in South Korea?
Seven days is ideal for a first-time south korea itinerary 7 days covering Seoul and Busan. Ten days adds Jeju Island comfortably. If you only have 5 days, stay entirely in Seoul and take one day trip - either DMZ or Nami Island - rather than rushing to Busan.
Is 7 days enough for Seoul, Busan, and Jeju?
Not really. A Seoul-Busan-Jeju itinerary in 7 days is too rushed - too much time is spent in transit. Use your 7-day South Korea itinerary for Seoul and Busan only, and add Jeju to a 10-day trip instead.
What is the cheapest way to travel between Seoul and Busan?
The KTX high-speed train (KRW 59,800, 2.5 hours) is the most practical option. Budget airlines sometimes offer cheaper fares but require airport transit time on both ends. For most travelers on a South Korea 7 day travel plan where time is limited, the KTX is the better choice.
Is South Korea expensive for Singaporean travelers?
Mid-range - comparable to or slightly cheaper than Singapore for accommodation and local restaurant food. Convenience store meals (GS25, CU) are genuinely good at KRW 5,000-8,000 (SGD 5-8). Eating at tourist restaurants near major sights costs considerably more.
Do I need a visa for South Korea?
Singapore and Malaysia passport holders receive 90 days visa-free. Philippines passport holders currently receive 30 days visa-free - verify current policy before booking as this has changed periodically.
What is the best time to visit South Korea?
Spring (late March to early May) for cherry blossoms - the most popular and most crowded period. Autumn (October to November) for foliage and slightly lower prices. A Korea winter itinerary (December to February) offers snow scenery and the lowest hotel prices of the year.
