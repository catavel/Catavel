Thailand Travel Budget Guide 2026 - Daily Costs

Exactly how much Thailand costs - by city, by day, by trip length.

Planning your thailand travel budget correctly can mean the difference between a comfortable trip and one where you are constantly checking your wallet. Thailand is consistently one of the best-value travel destinations in Asia - but the budget for Bangkok differs significantly from the budget for Phuket or Chiang Mai. A daily thailand travel budget of THB 1,200 gets you a comfortable private room, three excellent street food meals, and public transport in Bangkok. The same THB 1,200 in Phuket barely covers a beachfront lunch.

This guide breaks down the travel budget for Thailand by destination, with honest numbers so you can plan a trip to Thailand on a budget that actually works.

How to Save Money on Your Thailand Travel Budget

  • Eat street food and local restaurants - pad thai and khao man gai from THB 40-70 (SGD 1.60-2.80)
  • Use BTS/MRT in Bangkok - a Rabbit card saves per-ride versus single tickets
  • Book activities via Klook rather than hotel concierge or street operators (10-30% cheaper)
  • Use Agoda Secret Deals for hotels - log in to unlock rates 15-30% below published prices
  • Travel shoulder season (April to May, September to October) for 20-40% lower accommodation
  • Get an eSIM before departure - Airalo Thailand plans from $4.50/1GB versus THB 299-399 for a tourist SIM at the airport
  • Avoid Songkran (mid-April) when hotel prices double and Bangkok is extremely busy

Thailand vs Other SEA Destinations - Value Comparison

How does the thailand travel budget compare against other popular SEA destinations from Singapore?

Thailand vs Bali: Comparable total budgets for a beach-focused trip. Thailand has better street food value and more developed transport infrastructure. Bali has better villa accommodation at the mid-range price point. Our Bali flights guide covers the SG to Bali cost comparison in detail.

Thailand vs Vietnam: Vietnam is cheaper overall - food costs 20-30% below Bangkok. Trade-off: less developed tourist infrastructure outside major cities.

Thailand vs Malaysia: Malaysia is the easiest and cheapest destination from Singapore. But Thailand's food scene and beach options are unmatched in the region - and the thailand travel budget for a full week remains very reasonable versus most global destinations.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much money do I need for 7 days in Thailand?
For a 7-day Bangkok and Chiang Mai trip from Singapore: SGD 500-800 total including flights. Budget breakdown: flights SGD 180-300, accommodation SGD 140-280 (7 nights), food SGD 80-130, transport SGD 50-90, activities SGD 60-100. Eating street food and using public transport keeps the in-country thailand travel budget to SGD 80-120 per day.
Is Thailand cheaper than Bali for a holiday?
For food and transport, yes - Bangkok is cheaper than any Bali resort area. For accommodation, they are comparable at the mid-range level. Overall trip budgets for similar lengths tend to be similar, with Thailand having a slight edge due to cheaper internal flights and better public transport.
What is the cheapest month to visit Thailand?
September and October (low season, some rain) have the lowest accommodation prices - often 30-50% below peak. May is also good value. Avoid Songkran (mid-April) when prices spike across the country.
Can I travel Thailand on SGD 50 per day?
In Bangkok and Chiang Mai, yes - just barely. SGD 50/day (approximately THB 1,250) covers a hostel dorm, three street food meals, BTS transport, and one paid attraction. SGD 60-70/day is a more realistic minimum thailand travel budget for a comfortable experience with some flexibility.
Should I bring cash or use cards in Thailand?
Both. Street food, markets, and older businesses are cash-only. Thailand ATMs charge a THB 220 withdrawal fee per transaction - withdraw large amounts less frequently. Exchange SGD to THB in Singapore (Mustafa Centre or Arcade in Change Alley) for better rates than Bangkok airport counters.
