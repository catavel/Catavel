Things to Do in Batam 2026 - Complete Guide from Singapore

45 minutes from Singapore. A lot more to do than you think.

Batam is the most visited Indonesian island you have probably never properly researched. Most Singaporeans treat it as a spontaneous day trip rather than a planned destination - and that is a missed opportunity. There are genuine things to do in Batam beyond the seafood and shopping that most first-timers discover, and knowing what to look for makes the difference between a forgettable trip and a genuinely satisfying weekend away.

This guide covers the best things to do in Batam for Singaporeans in 2026: how to get there efficiently, what to see, where to eat, how to shop, and what Batam things to do with kids. It also settles the Batam vs Bintan question for those deciding between the two nearest Indonesian island getaways from Singapore.

Getting to Batam from Singapore

The ferry from Singapore to Batam runs from two terminals: HarbourFront Ferry Terminal (ferries to Batam Centre and Sekupang) and Tanah Merah Ferry Terminal (ferries to Nongsapura). The crossing takes 45 minutes to 1 hour depending on route and sea conditions.

Book your Batam ferry via Klook for the most convenient experience - e-tickets go directly to your phone, and Klook pricing is typically at or below counter price. Weekday ferries (Monday to Thursday) are significantly cheaper than weekend departures. One-way fares run SGD 18-35 depending on timing and operator. Book the night before rather than buying at the terminal on the day.

Essential Batam preparation: bring your passport (Indonesian immigration is required even for day trips), exchange some SGD to IDR at the moneychanger inside the ferry terminal (better rates than Singapore), and sort your data before boarding. Best Things to Do in Batam - Activities and Attractions

The top things to do in Batam extend well beyond the malls most first-timers gravitate toward. There is genuine Batam sightseeing to be done if you venture beyond Nagoya.

Barelang Bridge is the most iconic photo spot in Batam - a chain of six bridges connecting the island to the smaller islands of Rempang and Galang. The approach from Batam gives a dramatic view of the structure against the open sea. The area around the bridge has seafood restaurants and local snack vendors. Arrange a driver for a half-day Barelang tour, or book a guided Batam day tour on Klook that includes the bridge alongside other key Batam attractions.

Maha Vihara Duta Maitreya Temple near Nagoya is one of the largest Buddhist temples in Southeast Asia, featuring a 30-meter Maitreya Buddha statue visible from a considerable distance. Entry is free and the grounds are open to all visitors. Respectful dress is expected - cover shoulders and knees.

Nongsa Coast in the northeast of Batam is a different world from the commercial center. Beach clubs, the Nongsa Point Marina, and a stretch of coastline that feels genuinely resort-like. Most beach clubs charge IDR 50,000-100,000 day entry, typically redeemable against food and drinks orders.

Mega Wisata Ocarina is a family theme park near Batam Centre with rides and water attractions suitable for children under 12. Entry is IDR 100,000-150,000 depending on height. Check our Klook promo codes for any available discounts on Batam tours and activity bundles.

Best Food in Batam

Food is the main event for most Singaporeans visiting Batam, and prices are a fraction of Singapore's for comparable quality. The seafood in particular represents extraordinary value.

Golden Prawn Restaurant in Nagoya has been a Singaporean institution for decades - live seafood from tanks priced by weight, cooked in your choice of style (pepper, salted egg, butter, or chili). A full meal for two with multiple dishes and drinks costs around SGD 25-40, versus SGD 80-120 for the equivalent in Singapore. Other well-regarded seafood spots include Ocean King in Batam Centre and the waterfront restaurants in Sekupang.

Mie Tarempa is something you cannot easily find in Singapore - a distinct Batam noodle dish with fish cake, chili, and a rich broth that has been a local staple for generations. The best versions are at small shops around Nagoya, and a bowl costs IDR 20,000-35,000 (SGD 1.80-3). Indonesian street food staples - nasi campur, soto ayam, martabak - are available throughout the Nagoya food stall area at similar prices.

Budget for food in Batam: SGD 5-15 per meal eating local, SGD 20-40 for a proper seafood feast for two. It is genuinely difficult to overspend on food in Batam if you eat at local and mid-range restaurants rather than tourist-facing hotel dining.

Shopping in Batam

Batam's shopping reputation is built on a specific set of categories where it genuinely beats Singapore on price. Indonesian snacks and groceries are the primary draw: Indomie varieties unavailable in Singapore, local crackers and krupuk, coconut products, sambal pastes, and traditional Riau snacks. The basement supermarket at Nagoya Hill Mall has the widest range, all significantly cheaper than Singapore equivalents.

Indonesian batik fabric and traditional handicrafts from the stalls inside Nagoya Hill are often genuine and reasonably priced - good for gifts. Singapore customs allowances for Indonesia purchases: declare items over SGD 500 in total value. Duty-free allowance for alcohol is 1 liter for Singapore residents returning from Indonesia.

Family-Friendly Things to Do in Batam

Things to do in Batam with family have expanded significantly in recent years. Beyond Ocarina Theme Park, Funtasy Island - a waterpark and beach resort accessible by speedboat from Batam - offers a full family resort day that works well for children of all ages. Check current availability and package pricing via Klook.

Montigo Resorts Nongsa and Turi Beach Resort both have excellent family facilities including pools, kids clubs, and beach access. Book via Agoda - Secret Deals pricing on these properties can be 20-30% below the resort's direct booking rate. Go-Kart tracks near Nagoya are popular with older children and teens at IDR 30,000-50,000 per lap.

For families combining things to do in Batam with a resort stay, the Nongsa peninsula offers the best combination of beach access, family facilities, and relative quiet compared to the commercial Nagoya area. Factor in a half-day for Barelang Bridge and seafood lunch at Golden Prawn to get the full Batam experience alongside the resort day.

Batam vs Bintan - Which to Choose?

Choose Batam if you want: cheap seafood, shopping, massages, or a budget day trip that requires minimal planning. Batam is commercial, busy, and optimized for visitors who want value and convenience. A satisfying Batam day trip costs SGD 80-150 total - the best-value Indonesian getaway from Singapore by a considerable margin.

Choose Bintan if you want: a beach resort experience, cleaner beaches, a quieter atmosphere, and do not mind spending significantly more. Bintan is oriented around resort stays rather than day trips. Frequently Asked Questions

Is Batam safe for Singaporean tourists?
Yes. The main tourist areas (Nagoya, Batam Centre, Nongsa) are well-traveled with strong tourist infrastructure. Standard precautions apply: use Grab rather than negotiating with informal taxis, keep valuables secure in busy areas, and book accommodation via Agoda or Klook for reviewed properties with clear cancellation policies.
Do I need a visa for Batam from Singapore?
Singapore passport holders receive 30 days visa-free entry to Indonesia including Batam. Malaysian and most other SEA passport holders are also visa-free for 30 days. Your passport must have at least 6 months validity and a blank page for the entry stamp. There is no way to enter without a passport - this is a firm requirement regardless of trip length.
Can I use Singapore dollars in Batam?
Yes, SGD is widely accepted at tourist-facing businesses, restaurants, and most shops in Nagoya. For the best value, exchange SGD to IDR at the licensed moneychanger inside the ferry terminal rather than at point of sale. Rates at moneychangers are consistently 3-5% better than the implicit rate when paying in SGD directly.
What is the best day to visit Batam to avoid crowds?
Tuesday to Thursday are consistently the least crowded and have the cheapest ferry fares. Weekend departures - especially Friday afternoon and Saturday morning - are busy with Singaporeans and the HarbourFront terminal can be congested. Midweek visits also mean shorter queues at popular restaurants like Golden Prawn.
How long is the ferry from Singapore to Batam?
The crossing takes approximately 45 minutes to 1 hour depending on the route and sea conditions. HarbourFront to Batam Centre is typically 45-50 minutes. Allow extra time for check-in and immigration on both sides - arrive at the Singapore terminal 30-45 minutes before departure, especially on weekends when queues are longer.
