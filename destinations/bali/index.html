Where to Stay in Bali

Bali accommodation ranges from USD 10/night hostels to USD 500+ luxury resorts. The best area depends on what you want: Seminyak and Canggu for beach clubs and nightlife, Ubud for rice terraces and culture, Nusa Dua for family-friendly resorts, and Uluwatu for clifftop views and surfing.

Getting to Bali from Singapore & Malaysia

Bali's Ngurah Rai International Airport (DPS) is well-connected to both Singapore Changi (SIN) and Kuala Lumpur (KUL). Direct flights take around 3 hours and budget carriers like Scoot, AirAsia, and Jetstar frequently offer fares under SGD 150 return. Frequently Asked Questions

Is Bali worth visiting from Singapore?
Bali is one of the most affordable and accessible destinations from Singapore. With direct flights under 3.5 hours and return fares frequently dropping below SGD 150 on budget carriers, it offers exceptional value. Activities, food, and accommodation are significantly cheaper than Singapore, making it ideal for weekend getaways and week-long holidays alike.
How much should I budget for a Bali trip?
For a comfortable mid-range trip from Singapore: SGD 150-250 for return flights, USD 30-80/night for a good hotel, and USD 10-30 per activity. A 5-day trip for two people typically costs SGD 1,200-2,500 total including flights, hotel, activities, and meals. Budget travelers can do it for under SGD 800 per person.

Do I need a visa for Bali?
Singapore and Malaysia passport holders can enter Indonesia visa-free for stays up to 30 days. For stays longer than 30 days, you can purchase a Visa on Arrival (VOA) at the airport for IDR 500,000 (approximately SGD 45), which is extendable for another 30 days.
When is the best time to visit Bali?
The dry season (April to October) offers the best weather with minimal rain and lower humidity. July-August is peak season with higher prices. For the best balance of good weather and lower prices, visit in April-June or September-October. The wet season (November-March) brings afternoon showers but also lower prices and fewer crowds.
