Where to Stay in Bangkok

Bangkok accommodation is exceptional value — quality hotels start from USD 25/night. Sukhumvit (near BTS Nana to Phrom Phong) is best for nightlife and shopping, Silom for business travelers, Khao San Road for backpackers, and Riverside for luxury hotels with Chao Phraya views. Getting to Bangkok from Singapore & Malaysia

Bangkok's Suvarnabhumi Airport (BKK) is one of the best-connected hubs in Asia. Direct flights from Singapore take around 2.5 hours, and from KL about 2.25 hours. Budget carriers like Scoot, AirAsia, and VietJet frequently offer return fares under SGD 150. Check our flight deals page for the latest discounted fares — our pipeline scans prices every 6 hours.

💡 Bangkok Travel Tips for SEA Travelers

  • eSIM: Get a travel eSIM before you fly — Thailand has fast 4G/5G coverage everywhere. See our eSIM comparison guide.
  • BTS/MRT: Bangkok's skytrain and metro cover all major tourist areas. Buy a Rabbit card for tap-and-go convenience — much faster than taxis in rush hour.
  • Cash: Street food vendors and small shops are cash-only. ATMs are everywhere but charge 220 THB per withdrawal. Bring a Wise card for the best rate.
  • Grab: Works perfectly in Bangkok. Use GrabCar (not regular taxis) to avoid meter scams, especially from tourist areas.
  • Dress code: Temples require covered shoulders and knees. Carry a light sarong or long pants — you'll be turned away at the Grand Palace without them.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Bangkok a good weekend trip from Singapore?
Bangkok is one of the most popular weekend destinations from Singapore. With a 2.5-hour direct flight, you can depart Friday evening and be in Sukhumvit by dinner. Return fares regularly drop below SGD 120 on budget carriers. A 2-3 night trip gives enough time for temples, street food, shopping, and a spa day.
How much should I budget for a Bangkok trip?
Bangkok is very affordable. Budget SGD 100-200 for return flights, USD 25-60/night for a good hotel, and most activities cost under USD 30. Street food meals cost USD 1-3. A comfortable 3-night trip for two people typically costs SGD 800-1,500 total including everything.

Do I need a visa for Bangkok?
Singapore passport holders get visa-free entry to Thailand for up to 60 days. Malaysian passport holders also get visa-free entry for up to 30 days. No advance visa required for short trips — just show your passport at immigration.
When is the best time to visit Bangkok?
The cool and dry season from November to February offers the most pleasant weather with temperatures around 25-32°C. March to May is hot season (35°C+). June to October is rainy season — still fine for visiting, but expect afternoon thunderstorms. Songkran (Thai New Year, mid-April) is exciting but hotels book up fast.
