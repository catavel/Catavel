1Victoria Peak Tram + Sky Terrace 428
⭐ 4.5 (20,000+ reviews) ⏱ 2-3 hours 🏔️ Iconic viewpoint

The most iconic Hong Kong experience - ride the historic Peak Tram (running since 1888) up to Victoria Peak for a jaw-dropping panorama of the harbour, Kowloon, and the surrounding islands. The Sky Terrace 428 at the top is Hong Kong's highest observation deck at 428 metres above sea level. Visit at sunset for the best light. Where to Stay in Hong Kong

Tsim Sha Tsui (Kowloon) is the best base for first-timers - harbour views, great shopping, and easy MTR access to everything. Central and Causeway Bay are premium locations on Hong Kong Island. Mong Kok is budget-friendly with incredible street food and markets. Hotels start from HKD 400/night for budget options up to HKD 3,000+ for harbour-view luxury. Check Trip.com for the best HK hotel deals - they dominate this market.

Getting to Hong Kong from Singapore & Malaysia

Hong Kong International Airport (HKG) is a major hub served by every major airline. Direct flights from Singapore take about 3.5-4 hours. Scoot, Cathay Pacific, and Singapore Airlines all fly the route. From KL, AirAsia and Cathay Pacific operate direct flights in about 3.5 hours. Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hong Kong worth visiting from Singapore?
Hong Kong offers a completely different experience from Singapore - denser, grittier, with arguably the best food scene in Asia. With a 4-hour direct flight and no visa required for Singaporeans (up to 90 days), it's an easy 3-5 night trip. The theme parks (Disneyland's Frozen land, Ocean Park) and the harbour skyline are world-class. Shopping is also cheaper than SG for many electronics and fashion brands.
How many days do I need in Hong Kong?
Minimum 3 nights, ideally 4-5. Day 1: Victoria Peak + Central district. Day 2: Disneyland or Ocean Park. Day 3: Lantau Island (Ngong Ping 360 + Big Buddha + Tai O). Day 4: Mong Kok markets + dim sum + harbour cruise. Day 5: Day trip to Macau (1 hour by ferry). Hong Kong is compact - you can cover a lot in a short trip.
Do I need a visa for Hong Kong?
Singapore passport holders get visa-free entry for up to 90 days. Malaysian passport holders also get visa-free entry for up to 90 days. No advance visa required - just a valid passport.
Should I use Klook or Trip.com for Hong Kong?

Trip.com is often better for hotels and flights - they're a Hong Kong-based company with especially deep local inventory. Check both platforms and use promo codes from our voucher pages to get the best deal on each booking.
When is the best time to visit Hong Kong?
October to December is ideal - cool, dry weather with clear skies (best for Victoria Peak views). January-February is cool but can be grey. March-April is pleasant but foggy. Summer (June-September) is hot, humid, and typhoon season - cheapest for flights but least comfortable. Chinese New Year (January/February) is festive but very crowded.
