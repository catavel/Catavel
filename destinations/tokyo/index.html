Where to Stay in Tokyo

Tokyo accommodation varies wildly — from USD 30/night capsule hotels to USD 500+ luxury ryokans. Shinjuku is the best base for first-timers (central, great transport links, nightlife). Shibuya for trendier vibes, Asakusa for traditional atmosphere, and Ginza for luxury shopping. For hotel deals, check Agoda or Trip.com for flight + hotel bundles from SIN or KUL.

Getting to Tokyo from Singapore & Malaysia

Tokyo has two airports: Narita (NRT, 60-90 min from city centre) and Haneda (HND, 30 min, preferred). Direct flights from Singapore take about 7 hours. Scoot flies direct to Narita, while Singapore Airlines and ANA serve Haneda. From KL, AirAsia X flies to Narita. Return fares range from SGD 250-500 depending on season.   • eSIM: Japan has excellent 4G/5G coverage. Get a travel eSIM before departure — much easier than pocket WiFi. See our eSIM comparison guide.
  • IC card: Get a Suica or Pasmo card at the airport for tap-and-go train, bus, and convenience store payments. Way more convenient than buying individual tickets.
  • Cash: Japan is still heavily cash-based, especially at smaller restaurants and shrines. Withdraw yen at 7-Eleven ATMs (lowest fees). Bring a Wise card for the best exchange rate.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Tokyo expensive for Singapore travelers?
Tokyo is more expensive than Southeast Asian destinations like Bali or Bangkok, but with the weak yen (as of 2025-2026), it offers surprisingly good value. Street food costs USD 3-8, a good ramen is USD 7-10, and budget hotels start from USD 30/night. Activities on Klook range from USD 7-60. A 5-day Tokyo trip for two people typically costs SGD 3,000-5,000 total.
How many days do I need in Tokyo?
Minimum 4 days, ideally 5-7. Day 1: Asakusa + Akihabara. Day 2: Shibuya + Harajuku + Shinjuku. Day 3: teamLab + Toyosu. Day 4: DisneySea or Mt Fuji day trip. Day 5+: Kamakura, Nikko, or deeper neighbourhood exploration. Tokyo rewards slow exploration — you'll always find something new.
Do I need a visa for Japan?
Singapore passport holders get visa-free entry for up to 90 days. Malaysian passport holders also get visa-free entry for up to 90 days (as of 2024 reinstatement). No advance visa required for tourism — just a valid passport with at least 6 months validity.
When is the best time to visit Tokyo?
Cherry blossom season (late March to mid-April) is the most magical but also the busiest and most expensive. Autumn (October-November) offers stunning fall colours with fewer crowds. Summer (June-August) is hot and humid. Winter (December-February) is cold but clear — best for Mt Fuji views and lower prices. Year-round, Tokyo is always worth visiting.
