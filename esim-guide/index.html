Best eSIM for Travel 2026

Compare Airalo, Yesim, Saily, and Drimsim - the top travel eSIM providers for Southeast Asia travelers. Stop paying roaming fees and get connected for a fraction of the cost.

Travel Essential

Ditch the SIM card. Get an eSIM.

An eSIM is a digital SIM that lets you connect to local networks without swapping physical cards. Install before you fly, activate when you land - maps, Grab, and WhatsApp working before you clear immigration.

Why this matters: Carrier roaming typically costs SGD 10-20/day. A travel eSIM costs SGD 3-8 for an entire trip. That's a saving of SGD 50-150+ per person per trip. Every traveler needs one - it's the single easiest way to cut travel costs.

💡 Pro tip: Install before you fly

Always install your eSIM at home on WiFi before departure. You can leave it turned off until you land. This way you have internet the moment you step off the plane - no hunting for airport WiFi or SIM card counters.

Is Your Phone eSIM Compatible?

Most phones from 2019 onwards support eSIM. Here are the most common eSIM-compatible phones for SEA travelers:

  • iPhone: XS, XR, and all newer models (11, 12, 13, 14, 15, 16 series)
  • Samsung: Galaxy S20 and newer, Galaxy Z Flip/Fold series
  • Google Pixel: Pixel 3 and newer
  • Huawei: P40, Mate 40 series (limited carrier support)

To check: dial *#06# on your phone. If an EID number appears, your phone supports eSIM. Also make sure your phone is carrier-unlocked - phones bought on contract may be locked to your home network.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use eSIM and my regular SIM at the same time?
Yes. Most modern phones support dual SIM - your physical SIM stays active for calls and texts on your home number, while the eSIM handles data. You choose which line to use for data in your phone settings. This is the recommended setup for travelers.
Do I need to remove my physical SIM card?
No. The eSIM works alongside your physical SIM. You don't need to remove or swap anything. Just set the eSIM as your data line and keep your physical SIM for calls/texts.
What if I run out of data?
All four providers let you top up or buy additional data through their app. With Saily and Airalo, you can purchase a new plan instantly. Drimsim auto-charges from your balance. Always buy a plan with slightly more data than you think you need - it's cheap insurance.
Can I use my eSIM as a hotspot?
It depends on the provider and plan. Yesim fully supports hotspot/tethering on all plans. Airalo supports it on most plans but check the specific plan details. Saily has limited hotspot support. If hotspot is essential (e.g., sharing data with a laptop), Yesim is the best choice.
Is eSIM safe and private?
Yes - eSIM connections use the same encrypted cellular networks as physical SIM cards. Saily goes a step further with a built-in VPN that encrypts your data even on public WiFi. For banking apps and sensitive browsing while traveling, Saily's VPN is a genuine security advantage.
Which eSIM is best for Bali / Thailand / Japan?
For single-country trips to any of these destinations, Saily offers the cheapest per-GB pricing. For a multi-country Asia trip (e.g., Thailand + Japan + Korea), Airalo's Asia regional plan is the most convenient option - one eSIM covers the entire trip.
